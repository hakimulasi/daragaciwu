Early Life

Freddie Highmore was raised in a family closely rooted in showbiz in Camden, London having been born in 1992 to Edward Highmore, an actor himself and Sue Latimer, a talent agent largely credited for kick-starting the careers of many popular actors over the years, including Imelda Staunton and Daniel Radcliffe. Freddie is the eldest of the two children born to his parents and his younger brother, Bertie is also an actor.

Freddie’s primary education began at the Hampstead Garden Suburb in the Hampstead village of London. He later attended an independent school called the Highgate School before enrolling at the Emmanuel College at Cambridge from where he graduated in Spanish and Arabic.

Freddie Highmore shares a deep bond with Vera Farmiga, his fellow actor on “Bates Motel”. In fact, he is the godfather of Vera’s son, Fynn. He is also a polyglot, for he can speak many other languages apart from of course English. He currently resides in Highgate, North London.

Career

Freddie Highmore began his TV career when he was just seven years old. In 1999, he made his film debut by featuring in a comedy film, “Women Talking Dirty” directed under the banner of Coky Giedroyc. In 2000, he played the role of King Arthur in “The Mists of Avalon” miniseries of TNT cable. In 2001, in the “Happy Birthday Shakespeare” miniseries of BBC, he played the role of a young boy who wishes to move his family to the market town of Stratford-upon-Avon, England. In 2004, he worked in Jean-Jacques Annaud’s film, “Two Brothers”.

In 2005, he played the role of Charlie Bucket in the film, “Charlie and the Chocolate Factory” directed by Tim Burton. In 2006, he portrayed a young Max Skinner in the comedy film, “A Good Year” directed by Ridley Scott. In 2007, he worked in the film, “August Rush” along with other stars, like Jonathan Rhys Meyers, Keri Russell and Robin Williams. In the 2008 film, “The Spiderwick Chronicles”, he played the double role of twins, Jared Grace and Simon. He voiced for the main character in the animated film, “Astro Boy” released in 2009.

In the 2010 film, “Master Harold…and the Boys”, he appeared opposite Ving Rhames. In 2011, he acted with Emma Roberts in the film, “The Art of Getting By”. From 2013-2017, he played the role of Norman Bates with his acting mother Vera Farmiga as Norma in the horror TV series, “Bates Motel” of A&E TV channel.
