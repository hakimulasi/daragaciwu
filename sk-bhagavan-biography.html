SK Bhagavan (born 3 July 1933 – 20 February 2023; Aged: 89 years old) was a talented senior director, media face, former actor, and internet personality from Mysore, Karnataka, India. He was famously known in the country for serving several decades of his life in the Indian film industry.

Who was Senior Director SK Bhagavan?

According to the official reports, he entered the film industry with the purpose to be an actor but lesser he know that he will fell in love with directing. Initially, he worked as an assistant to Kanagal Prabhakar Shastri for the film ‘Bhagyodaya’ in 1956. Later, he decided to start his own directorial projects. After directing a few movies, he collaborated with a filmmaker named B. Dorai Raj.

They become the most famous filmmaking duo in India because of their skills. The titles of their movies are Sandhya Raga, Rajadurgada Rahasya, Jedara Bale, Kasturi Nivasa, Eradu Kanasu, Operation Diamond Racket, Gaali Maathu, Hosa Belaku, Yarivanu, Hennina Koogu, Gagana, Jeevana Chaitra, and many more.

Directing Career and Profession

  • According to the sources, Srinivas Krishna Iyengar Bhagavan had an interest in acting and performed in plays for ‘Hirannayya Mitra Mandi’. Later, in the 1956 film Bhagyodaya, he got his start in the film industry as an assistant to Kanagal Prabhakar Shastri.
  • After that, he directed his first movie, Sandhya Raga, in 1966, though AC Narasimha Murthy was officially credited with directing it.
  • However, the following year, he and AC Narasimha Murthy were credited as co-directors of the 1967 film Rajadurgada Rahasya. When he co-directed Jedara Bale (1968) with Dorai Raj under the name Dorai–Bhagavan, he made his official directorial debut.
  • As the first Kannada directors to make films in the James Bond style, they were also the first. After that, they directed numerous films, the majority of which were based on Kannada novels. Their films were exceptionally effective in the film industry.
  • Gaalimaatu, Chandanada Gombe, Hosa Belaku, Kasturi Nivasa, Eradu Kanasu, Bayaludaari, Benkiya Bale, Jeevana Chaitra, and others are among the films. He has also collaborated with actors Lakshmi, Anant Nag, and others.
  • In 1996, Bhagavan and Dorai worked together on their final film, ‘Baalondu Chaduranga’. He stopped directing after B Dorai Raj passed away. Finally, he directed his final film, Aduva Gombe, which was his 50th directorial effort.
