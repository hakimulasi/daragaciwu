Early Life and Career Beginnings

Born Desariee Starr Compton on February 21, 1989, in Long Beach, California, Scout grew up with a unique connection to the horror genre, thanks to her father being a mortician.

Initially a competitive swimmer with dreams of the AAU Junior Olympics, she shifted gears to pursue acting, starting with roles in A.W.O.L. and Thursday Afternoon in 1998.

Breakthrough Roles and Major Projects

Scout’s big break came with her portrayal of Laurie Strode in Rob Zombie’s Halloween (2007) and its sequel Halloween II (2009).

These roles cemented her status in the horror genre. She also made a significant impact with her performance in The Runaways, where she played Lita Ford, alongside Kristen Stewart and Dakota Fanning.

Television Appearances and Recurring Roles

Scout has made numerous appearances on TV, including recurring roles in Gilmore Girls as Clara Forester and Charmed as various fairies. She also guest-starred in popular series like NCIS: Los Angeles and Grey’s Anatomy.

Awards and Recognitions

In 2007, Scout won the Method Fest Best Actress Award for her role in Tomorrow Is Today. This recognition highlighted her versatility and talent, proving her capability beyond the horror genre.
