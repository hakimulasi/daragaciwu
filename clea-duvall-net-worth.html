FAQs about Clea DuVall

Who is Clea DuVall?

Clea Helen D’Etienne DuVall, born on September 25, 1977, in Los Angeles, California, is an American actress and filmmaker known for her roles in The Faculty, Girl, Interrupted, and Argo.

Is she related to Robert DuVall or Shelley DuVall?

No, she is not related to veteran actors Robert DuVall or Shelley DuVall.

What are some of her notable film appearances?

Some of her notable films include The Faculty (1998), But I’m a Cheerleader (1999), Girl, Interrupted (1999), 21 Grams (2003), and Argo (2012).

What television roles has she played?

DuVall starred as Emma Borden in Lizzie Borden Took an Ax and its miniseries spinoff, The Lizzie Borden Chronicles. She also appeared in Carnivàle, Heroes, American Horror Story, Veep, and The Handmaid’s Tale.

What directorial work has Clea DuVall done?

DuVall directed the features The Intervention (2016) and Happiest Season (2020). She also created, wrote, and executive produced the Amazon Freevee series High School (2022–present).

Is she married?

Yes, she is married to Mia Weier. They keep their relationship private.

What advocacy work is Clea DuVall known for?

DuVall is an advocate for LGBTQ+ rights and has spoken out against discrimination. She is praised for her portrayal of lesbian characters in film and television.

How did she start her acting career?

DuVall made her screen debut in the low-budget horror film Little Witches (1996) and gained recognition with her role in The Faculty (1998).

What is she doing now?

Currently, DuVall voices Elsa on Fox’s HouseBroken, which she co-created. She continues to work as an actress, director, and producer.
