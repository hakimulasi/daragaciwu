Early Career in Los Angeles

Embarking on his journey in the bustling streets of Los Angeles, Doug Benson first stepped into the spotlight as a stand-in and extra in iconic films such as Blade Runner and Fast Times at Ridgemont High.

Benson’s career arc soared as he melded his comedic talent with his advocacy for marijuana rights, a unique blend that set him apart in the industry.

He released his first comedy album, Professional Humoredian, in 2008, and subsequently added several more to his repertoire, all resonating deeply with his growing audience.

His stand-up shows, characterized by a mix of satire, observational comedy, alternative comedy, and sarcasm, quickly became fan favorites.

When did Doug Benson start his stand-up comedy career?

What is he known for?

Benson is widely recognized for his love of marijuana, hosting popular podcasts like Doug Loves Movies, and starring in movies and TV shows. He’s also known for his Stoner Comedy style.

Has he appeared on other podcasts?

Yes, he has made appearances on numerous comedy podcasts, including Comedy Bang! Bang!, The Joe Rogan Experience, and WTF with Marc Maron.

What are some notable TV shows and movies he has appeared in?

Benson has appeared in a variety of television shows and movies, like @midnight, The Trailer Park Boys, and You’re The Worst. He also starred in the comedic court show The High Court with Doug Benson on Comedy Central.

Can you tell me about his podcast, Getting Doug with High?

From 2013 to 2019, Benson hosted the weekly talk show Getting Doug with High on YouTube, where he and his guests smoked marijuana and discussed various topics, often related to marijuana.

What is the documentary Super High Me about?

In the 2008 documentary Super High Me, he explored the effects of marijuana by not consuming it for 30 days and then consuming it excessively for another 30 days, revealing little to no negative health effects.

What is the concept of The Benson Interruption?

The Benson Interruption was a stand-up comedy show hosted by Benson, known for its unique format where he interjects with humorous commentary during other comedians’ sets.
