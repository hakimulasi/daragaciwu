FAQs about Bruno Canino

Who is Bruno Canino?

He is an acclaimed Italian pianist, chamber, and composer, renowned for his interpretations of both classical and contemporary works.

Where did Bruno Canino study music?

He studied piano at the Milan Conservatory and had Vincenzo Vitale as one of his earliest teachers.

What are some of  Canino’s significant professional roles?

Canino has been a piano teacher at the Milan Conservatory, the Bern Conservatory, and currently teaches at the Fiesole Music School.

Which orchestras and musicians has he worked with?

He has collaborated with numerous esteemed musicians including Itzhak Perlman and Viktoria Mullova, and has performed with major orchestras like the Berlin Philharmonic and the New York Philharmonic.

Has he received any awards for his music?

Yes, he won the Edison Prize for his duo recital with Viktoria Mullova, among other accolades.

What kind of music does he specialize in?

While he is well-versed in the classical repertoire, Canino is particularly noted for his advocacy and performance of contemporary music, having premiered works by composers such as Luciano Berio and Mauricio Kagel.

Does he write books or articles?

Yes, he authored Vademecum for a Chamber Pianist, which discusses the nuances of chamber music performance.

What recordings is Bruno Canino known for?

Notable recordings include Bach’s Goldberg Variations, the complete piano works of Alfredo Casella, and Debussy’s complete piano works.

Has he been dedicated any musical compositions?

Yes, several composers, including Wolfgang Rihm and Iannis Xenakis, have dedicated works to him.

Does Bruno Canino still perform?

Yes, he remains active as a performer, frequently participating in concerts and festivals across the world.

What are Canino’s contributions to music education?

Besides teaching, he regularly gives masterclasses internationally and has been involved in various educational programs and music festivals.

Is he involved in any music festivals?

He served as the musical director of the Venice Biennale from 1999 to 2002 and has been involved in other music organizations and festivals.
