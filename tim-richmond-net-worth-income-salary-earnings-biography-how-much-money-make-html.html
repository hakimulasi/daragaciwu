Tim Richmond Net Worth, Income, Salary, Earnings, Biography, How much money make?

Tim Richmond was an American racing car driver from Ashland, Ohio, who lived from June 7, 1955 until August 13, 1989. Before moving to NASCAR’s Winston Cup Series, he raced in the IndyCar Series (now Sprint Cup Series). Richmond was one of the first drivers to make the switch from open wheel racing to full-time NASCAR stock cars, which has since been the industry standard. During his eight NASCAR seasons, he won 13 races and was named Rookie of the Year at the 1980 Indianapolis 500. In 1986, Richmond had his best NASCAR season, finishing third in points. That season, he won seven races, more than any other driver on the tour. He was diagnosed with pneumonia after missing the season-opening Daytona 500 in February 1987. The infection was most likely caused by his impaired immune system as a consequence of AIDS. Despite his deteriorating health, Richmond contested in eight races in 1987, winning two of them and securing one pole position before retiring in August. Richmond tried to make a return in 1988 but was suspended by NASCAR after testing positive for a prohibited drug; when NASCAR demanded access to his whole medical record before restoring him, Richmond retired from racing.
