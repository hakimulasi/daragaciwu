What is the meaning of bahay kubo song?

It is literally translated as “Cubed House,” or “Cubed House.” The term derives from the time when American professors travelled to the Philippines to instruct students there. They were mesmerised by the square homes with just one room, which they thought were so unique. Nonetheless, this song is generally referred to as “Nipa Hut” due to the fact that these houses are constructed from nipa palm leaves and bamboo.

So, what exactly is the point of the song bahay kubo, you may wonder.

Bahay Kubo recounts the storey of a family that lives in a modest nipa hut surrounded by a vegetable garden overflowing with fresh produce. It is a song that expresses gratitude for the abundance of our land.
