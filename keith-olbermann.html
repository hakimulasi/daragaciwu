Career and Professional Life

Keith Olbermann is a sport and political pundit. Olbermann began his career at UPI and the RKO Radio Network before joining CNN in 1981 when the network was still in its early stages. As one of his first reports, he covered the 1980 Winter Olympics in Lake Placid, including the “Miracle on Ice.” In the early to mid-1980s, he worked as a sportscaster on the former WNEW 1130-AM radio station in New York City. Similarly, he briefly worked as a sports anchor at WCVB-TV in Boston in 1984 before heading to Los Angeles to work at KTLA and KCBS.

Olbermann joined ESPN’s SportsCenter in 1992 and remained there until 1997, with the exception of a brief spell at ESPN2 in 1993 and 1994. He became the network’s “marquee” personality to help launch ESPN2. On a regular basis, he and Dan Patrick co-hosted SportsCenter’s 11:00 p.m. program, and the two established a popular anchor combination. In 1995, Cable ACE awarded Olbermann the Best Sportscaster.

He and Patrick then co-wrote The Big Show, a book about their time at SportsCenter. Between 1997 and 2007, Olbermann wrote an essay titled “Mea Culpa” on Salon in November 2002, declaring, “I couldn’t handle the pressure of working in daily long-form television, and what was worse, I didn’t know I couldn’t handle it.”

Olbermann joined Fox Sports Net in 1999 as the host of the doomed daily sports news shows National Sports Report. Olbermann began providing twice-daily sports commentary on the ABC Radio Network after leaving Fox Sports in 2001, duplicating Howard Cosell’s “Speaking of Sports” and “Speaking of Everything” segments.
